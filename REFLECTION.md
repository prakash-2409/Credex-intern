# Reflection

## 1. What was the actual hardest bug?

- What was the exact error message or broken behavior?
- What did you think was causing it at first?
- What did you try that did not work?
- How did you finally fix it?

## 2. What changed about your design after user feedback?

- Which user comment or interview note mattered most?
- What part of the design did you change because of that feedback?
- What did you remove, simplify, or prioritize differently?
- Why was that change better for the actual workflow?

## 3. What did you learn about your users?

- What recurring pain point did you hear more than once?
- What surprised you about how they think about AI tool spend?
- What assumptions did you make that turned out to be wrong?
- What user quote best captures the main insight?

## 4. What would you do differently if you had one more week?

- What feature would you improve first and why?
- What part of the product would you simplify?
- What would you test with real users again?
- What would you stop spending time on?

## 5. What is still risky or incomplete?

- What logic, data, or content is still placeholder or manual?
- What could break in real use that the current tests do not catch?
- What should be verified before submission or deployment?
- What is the biggest remaining uncertainty?
